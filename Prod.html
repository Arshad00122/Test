Rice

We offer a diverse selection of high-quality, natural rice varieties, each uniquely suited to various culinary needs:

Basmati: Basmati is a long-grain rice known for its fragrant aroma and delicate, nutty flavor. Primarily grown in the Indian subcontinent, it is often used in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisines, particularly for dishes like biryani and pilaf. Basmati rice is prized for its slender grains that remain separate and fluffy when cooked.

Katarni Rice: Katarni rice is a lesser-known variety that is typically grown in specific regions. It is known for its short to medium grains and a somewhat sticky texture when cooked. This type of rice is often used in local traditional dishes where a cohesive, slightly glutinous texture is desired.

Sonam: Sonam rice is a popular variety in some regions of India, known for its medium to long grains and subtle aroma. It is versatile and can be used in a variety of dishes, from everyday meals to special recipes. Sonam rice often has a softer texture, making it ideal for both plain rice and elaborate recipes.

Vishnubhog: Vishnubhog is a premium variety of rice known for its fine grains, rich aroma, and distinct flavor. Grown in certain parts of India, it is often reserved for festive occasions and special dishes due to its superior quality and fragrant nature. Vishnubhog rice is often compared to basmati in terms of its delicate taste and texture.
